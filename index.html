Feijoa: The Green Gem

A celebration of my favourite fruit

What Is a Feijoa?

The feijoa (also known as pineapple guava) is a small green fruit with a fragrant, tropical aroma and a flavour unlike anything else. Native to South America and beloved in New Zealand and Australia, it’s a seasonal treat that people wait for all year.

Why I Love Feijoas

Feijoas are refreshing, aromatic, and incredibly unique. Their flavour blends hints of pineapple, guava, strawberry, and something floral that’s impossible to describe. They’re perfect eaten with a spoon, baked into desserts, or blended into smoothies.

Flavour & Texture

Inside the smooth green skin is a soft, jelly-like centre surrounded by firmer, grainy flesh. The taste is sweet, tangy, and intensely fragrant — a true sensory experience.

Where Feijoas Grow

Feijoas thrive in cooler subtropical climates. They grow widely in:

  • New Zealand
  • Australia
  • South America
  • Parts of California

How to Enjoy Feijoas

  • Eat fresh by scooping out the flesh
  • Blend into smoothies
  • Bake into cakes, muffins, or crumbles
  • Make chutneys, jams, or sauces
  • Freeze the pulp for later

Fun Facts

  • Feijoas fall from the tree when perfectly ripe.
  • The flowers are edible and taste sweet.
  • They’re rich in vitamin C and fibre.
  • Some people eat the skin for extra tang.
